Section 4A: Project Milestones and Acceptance

4A.1 Milestone Structure

Client's telehealth brand build is structured around four defined milestones. Upon completion of each milestone, Agency will deliver written notification to Client via email or Slack. Client has five (5) business days from delivery of written notification to review and provide written feedback or written approval.

The four milestones are:

  • Milestone 1: Brand Identity. Logo, color palette, typography, and brand guidelines delivered for Client review and approval.
  • Milestone 2: Website Design. Full website design mockups and copy delivered for Client review and approval prior to development commencement.
  • Milestone 3: Platform Configuration. WellieMD platform configured, patient portal live in staging environment, CRM setup complete, delivered for Client review and approval.
  • Milestone 4: Launch Approval. Full site live on Agency-managed hosting, all integrations active, test order completed, delivered for final Client sign-off prior to go-live.

4A.2 Deemed Acceptance

If Client does not provide written feedback or written rejection within five (5) business days of Agency's written milestone delivery notification, that milestone is deemed accepted and approved by Client. Deemed accepted milestones are considered complete, non-refundable, and may not be disputed as undelivered in any subsequent chargeback, dispute, or legal proceeding.

4A.3 Rejection and Revision

If Client provides written rejection within the five-day window, Agency will address the specific concerns raised and resubmit the milestone for a new five-day review period. The number of revision rounds per milestone is governed by Section 6 of Exhibit A. Rejection must be specific and in writing. General dissatisfaction without specific feedback does not constitute valid rejection.

4A.4 Milestone Records

Agency will maintain written records of all milestone delivery notifications and client acceptance responses. These records serve as evidence of delivery and acceptance in any dispute resolution proceeding. Client acknowledges that milestone acceptance records may be submitted to payment processors, financial institutions, or courts as evidence of services rendered.
